# Billing and Subscription

Stacktape offers three subscription plans — Free, Flexible, and Enterprise — managed through the Stacktape Console. Billing uses Paddle as the payment processor. You can view invoices, update payment details, and change plans from the Console without contacting support.

## What this part of the Console does

The billing and subscription area covers two Console pages: the **Subscription Plans** page (at `/subscription-plans`) where you choose or change your plan, and the **Billing** page (at `/billing`) where you view payment details and past invoices. Both pages require at least Admin-level access — Viewers and Members cannot see or manage billing.

## Subscription plans

Stacktape bills based on the AWS infrastructure costs your stacks generate — not per seat, per deployment, or per request. All plans include unlimited deployments and unlimited stacks.

### Free plan

The Free plan requires no credit card. It supports one team member and covers managed AWS infrastructure costs up to $100 per month. This is a good fit for individual developers, hobby projects, or evaluating Stacktape before committing to a paid plan.

### Flexible plan

The Flexible plan charges a percentage of your Stacktape-managed AWS infrastructure costs. Stacktape does not charge for other resources within your AWS account that are not managed by Stacktape. The percentage decreases as your spend increases:

| Monthly AWS costs (managed by Stacktape) | Stacktape fee |
|---|---|
| $100 – $1,000 | 30% |
| $1,000 – $4,000 | 28% |
| $4,000 – $10,000 | 26% |
| $10,000 – $15,000 | 24% |
| $15,000 – $25,000 | 22% |
| Greater than $25,000 | Contact sales |

The Flexible plan includes unlimited team members and hands-on support. Choose this plan when your team is growing or your AWS costs exceed the Free plan's $100/month cap.

### Enterprise plan

The Enterprise plan has custom pricing negotiated directly with the Stacktape team. It includes SSO, SOC 2 compliance, and up to 24x7 premium support. Schedule a meeting through the Console's subscription page to discuss pricing. Choose this when your organization requires compliance certifications, SSO integration, or dedicated support SLAs.

## Common tasks

### Viewing invoices

In the Stacktape Console, navigate to `/billing`. The **Invoices** section lists all past payments sorted by date (newest first). Each invoice shows a description, amount, tax, and a link to the receipt. If no payments have been made, the page displays "No invoices available."

### Updating payment details

On the `/billing` page, click **Update** next to your billing details. This opens the Paddle subscription management overlay where you can change your payment method, billing email, or address. This button is only visible to users with the `org:manage-billing` permission (Owner or Admin roles).

### Downgrading to the Free plan

Navigate to `/subscription-plans`. Under the Free plan card, click **Downgrade to FREE**. This cancels your Paddle subscription. After cancellation, your organization reverts to Free plan limits (1 team member, $100/month AWS cost cap). Existing stacks are not deleted, but you may not be able to deploy if you exceed plan limits.

### Viewing current billing details

The `/billing` page shows your current subscription information: customer name, billing email, currency, and subscription ID. If no active subscription exists (you're on the Free plan), the page shows "No active subscription."

## Permissions

Billing access is controlled by two permissions, distributed by organization role:

| Role | View billing | Manage billing |
|---|---|---|
| Owner | Yes | Yes |
| Admin | Yes | Yes |
| Developer | No | No |
| Member | No | No |
| Viewer | No | No |

- **View billing** (`org:view-billing`) — access the costs and usage page.
- **Manage billing** (`org:manage-billing`) — upgrade, downgrade, and update payment details.

Viewers are redirected to the overview page if they attempt to access billing or subscription pages directly.

## Troubleshooting

### Cannot see the Upgrade or Downgrade button

You need the Owner or Admin role in your organization. Ask your organization owner to either promote your role or handle the billing change. Developers, Members, and Viewers do not have billing management permissions.

### Payment completed but plan not updated

After a successful Paddle checkout, the Console polls for the updated subscription status over several seconds. If the plan still shows the old state, refresh the page. Paddle webhook delivery can occasionally take a few seconds to propagate.

### "No active subscription" on the billing page

This means your organization is on the Free plan with no Paddle subscription attached. This is normal — navigate to `/subscription-plans` to upgrade if needed.

## FAQ

### How does Stacktape pricing work?

Stacktape charges a percentage of the AWS infrastructure costs it manages for you. The Free plan covers up to $100/month with no fee. The Flexible plan charges a tiered percentage (30% down to 22%) that decreases as your managed AWS spend increases. Enterprise pricing is negotiated directly. Stacktape does not charge for AWS resources outside its management.

### Is there a per-deployment or per-request fee?

No. All plans include unlimited deployments and unlimited stacks. Pricing is based solely on AWS infrastructure costs managed by Stacktape, not on the number of deploys, API requests, or developer seats (on paid plans).

### What counts as "managed AWS costs"?

Managed AWS costs are the infrastructure expenses for resources defined in your Stacktape configuration and deployed through Stacktape — Lambda functions, containers, databases, buckets, load balancers, and similar. Resources in your AWS account that exist outside Stacktape-managed stacks are not counted toward Stacktape billing.

### Can I use Stacktape without a credit card?

Yes. The Free plan requires no credit card and supports one team member with up to $100/month in managed AWS costs. You still need an AWS account (billed separately by AWS), but Stacktape itself charges nothing on the Free tier.

### Who can manage billing in my organization?

Only users with the Owner or Admin role have the `org:manage-billing` permission. Developers, Members, and Viewers cannot view or change subscription plans. See [team and access control](/stacktape-console/team-and-access-control) for role management.

### What payment methods does Stacktape accept?

Stacktape uses Paddle as its payment processor. Paddle supports credit cards, debit cards, and PayPal in most regions. The specific payment options shown depend on your billing country and currency.

### What happens if I exceed the Free plan's $100/month limit?

If your Stacktape-managed AWS costs exceed $100/month on the Free plan, you need to upgrade to the Flexible plan to continue deploying. Existing infrastructure remains running, but new deployments may be restricted until you upgrade or reduce costs.

### Can I downgrade from Flexible to Free?

Yes. Navigate to `/subscription-plans` in the Console and click **Downgrade to FREE** under the Free plan card. This cancels your Paddle subscription. Be aware that the Free plan limits apply immediately (1 team member, $100/month AWS cost cap).

### How do I get Enterprise pricing?

On the `/subscription-plans` page, click the **Schedule a meeting** button under the Enterprise plan. This connects you with the Stacktape team to discuss custom pricing, SSO, SOC 2 compliance, and premium support options.

### Where can I find my invoices and receipts?

Navigate to `/billing` in the Stacktape Console. The invoices section lists all past payments with links to downloadable receipts. You need at least the Admin role to access this page.
