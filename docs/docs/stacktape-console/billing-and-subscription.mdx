# Billing and Subscription

Stacktape offers three subscription plans — Free, Flexible, and Enterprise — managed through the Stacktape Console. Billing uses Paddle as the payment processor. The Console lets you view invoices, change plans, and complete Paddle checkout from the billing pages.

## What this part of the Console does

The billing and subscription area covers two Console pages. The **Subscription Plans** page (at `/subscription-plans`) is where you choose or change your plan. The **Billing** page (at `/billing`) renders a billing details section, an invoices list, and a Paddle checkout container. Users with the VIEWER role are redirected to `/overview` when they attempt to access either page. On the subscription page, the **Upgrade now** and **Downgrade to FREE** buttons render only when the current user has the `org:manage-billing` permission. The `/billing` page passes that same `canManageBilling` flag to its billing details section.

## Subscription plans

Stacktape bills based on the AWS infrastructure costs your stacks generate — not per seat, per deployment, or per request. The Free and Flexible plan cards both list unlimited deployments and unlimited stacks.

### Free plan

The Free plan requires no credit card. It supports one team member and covers managed AWS infrastructure costs up to $100 per month. This is a good fit for individual developers, hobby projects, or evaluating Stacktape before committing to a paid plan. You still need your own AWS account (billed separately by AWS), but Stacktape itself charges nothing on this tier.

### Flexible plan

The Flexible plan charges a tiered percentage of your Stacktape-managed AWS infrastructure costs. The percentage decreases as your managed AWS spend increases — see the `/subscription-plans` page in the Console for current rates. Stacktape does not charge for resources in your AWS account that exist outside Stacktape-managed stacks.

The Flexible plan includes unlimited team members and hands-on support. Choose this when your team is growing or your AWS costs exceed the Free plan's $100/month cap.

### Enterprise plan

The Enterprise plan shows custom pricing on the subscription page and includes a schedule-meeting button to connect with the Stacktape team. The Enterprise plan card lists unlimited members, SSO, SOC 2 compliance, and up to 24x7 premium support. Choose this when your organization requires compliance certifications, SSO integration, or dedicated support SLAs.

## Walkthrough: upgrading to the Flexible plan

1. **Open subscription plans** — In the Stacktape Console, navigate to `/subscription-plans`.
2. **Review plan details** — Compare the three plans and their included features.
3. **Click Upgrade now** — Under the Flexible plan card, click the **Upgrade now** button. This starts the Paddle checkout flow. The page includes a Paddle checkout container for payment processing.
4. **Complete payment** — Enter your payment details in the Paddle checkout.
5. **Confirm activation** — On success, the Console refetches your organization data and updates the displayed plan status.

<Info>Only users with the `org:manage-billing` permission see the Upgrade and Downgrade buttons. If you don't see them, ask your organization owner to handle the billing change or adjust your role.</Info>

## Common tasks

### Viewing invoices

In the Stacktape Console, navigate to `/billing`. The page renders a billing details section and an invoices list showing past payments.

### Reviewing billing details

On the `/billing` page, the billing details section receives a `canManageBilling` flag derived from the `org:manage-billing` permission. Users without that permission can still view the page (unless they have the VIEWER role, in which case they are redirected to `/overview`).

### Downgrading to the Free plan

Navigate to `/subscription-plans`. Under the Free plan card, click **Downgrade to FREE**. This calls the Paddle subscription cancellation flow. On success, the Console refetches your organization data and updates the displayed plan. Free plan limits apply after downgrade (1 team member, $100/month managed AWS cost cap).

### Viewing current plan status

The `/subscription-plans` page shows which plan is currently active. The active plan displays a "Currently active" indicator instead of an upgrade or downgrade button.

## Permissions

Billing access is gated in two ways:

- **VIEWER redirect** — Users with the VIEWER role are redirected to `/overview` when they open `/billing` or `/subscription-plans`.
- **`org:manage-billing`** — The **Upgrade now** and **Downgrade to FREE** buttons on `/subscription-plans` are rendered only when the current user has this permission. The same `canManageBilling` flag is passed into the billing details section on `/billing`.

For the full role-to-permission mapping and how to change a user's role, see [team and access control](/stacktape-console/team-and-access-control).

<Warning>Users with the VIEWER role are automatically redirected to `/overview` when attempting to access billing or subscription pages.</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

### Cannot see the Upgrade or Downgrade button

You need the `org:manage-billing` permission. Ask your organization owner to adjust your role or handle the billing change. The buttons are conditionally rendered based on this permission.

### Plan not updating after payment

After a successful Paddle checkout, the Console refetches organization data and updates the selected organization from the response. If the plan still shows the old state, refresh the page manually. Paddle webhook delivery can occasionally take a few seconds.

### "No active subscription" on the billing page

This means your organization is on the Free plan with no Paddle subscription attached. This is normal — navigate to `/subscription-plans` to upgrade if needed.

## FAQ

### How does Stacktape pricing work?

Stacktape charges based on the AWS infrastructure costs it manages for you. The Free plan covers up to $100/month with no fee. The Flexible plan charges a tiered percentage that decreases as your managed AWS spend increases. Enterprise pricing is custom. Stacktape does not charge for AWS resources outside its management.

### Is there a per-deployment or per-request fee?

No. The Free and Flexible plan cards list unlimited deployments and unlimited stacks. Pricing is based on AWS infrastructure costs managed by Stacktape, not on the number of deploys, API requests, or developer seats (on paid plans).

### What counts as "managed AWS costs"?

Managed AWS costs are the infrastructure expenses for resources defined in your Stacktape configuration and deployed through Stacktape — Lambda functions, containers, databases, buckets, load balancers, and similar. Resources in your AWS account that exist outside Stacktape-managed stacks are not counted toward Stacktape billing.

### Can I use Stacktape without a credit card?

Yes. The Free plan requires no credit card and supports one team member with up to $100/month in managed AWS costs. You still need an AWS account (billed separately by AWS), but Stacktape itself charges nothing on the Free tier.

### Who can manage billing in my organization?

Users with the `org:manage-billing` permission see the upgrade and downgrade controls on `/subscription-plans`, and the same flag is passed into the billing details section on `/billing`. Users without this permission can still view the billing and subscription pages (except Viewers, who are redirected). See [team and access control](/stacktape-console/team-and-access-control) for role management.

### What payment methods does Stacktape accept?

Stacktape uses Paddle as its payment processor. Paddle supports credit cards, debit cards, and PayPal in most regions. The specific payment options shown depend on your billing country and currency.

### What happens if I exceed the Free plan's $100/month limit?

If your Stacktape-managed AWS costs exceed $100/month on the Free plan, you need to upgrade to the Flexible plan to continue using Stacktape's full capabilities. Navigate to `/subscription-plans` to upgrade.

### Can I downgrade from Flexible to Free?

Yes. Navigate to `/subscription-plans` in the Console and click **Downgrade to FREE** under the Free plan card. This cancels your Paddle subscription. Free plan limits apply after downgrade (1 team member, $100/month AWS cost cap).

### How do I get Enterprise pricing?

On the `/subscription-plans` page, click the schedule-meeting button under the Enterprise plan card. This connects you with the Stacktape team to discuss custom pricing, SSO, SOC 2 compliance, and premium support options.

### Where can I find my invoices?

Navigate to `/billing` in the Stacktape Console. The page includes an invoices list showing past payments. Users with the VIEWER role cannot access this page.
